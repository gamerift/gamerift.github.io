Game Description#

Ctrl Alt Ego uses a science fiction setting. The action takes place in the gloomy future. The plot is dedicated to the fate of human consciousness. The game is a first -person shooter and an immersion simulator. It must be controlled by the incorporeal ego of the protagonist. The main character can instill in robots and objects (for example, doors or computers). He cannot really die. In the case of the destruction of the membrane, the hero simply returns to the form of the spirit and does not lose progress. He can again take control of someone and continue to explore the card. If desired, you can persist and load the saves so as not to lose your favorite mechanical body and then not look for a new one.

There is nonlinearity in CAE. A video game encourages players to experiment. Users can entertain themselves in this sandbox on their own. They are allowed in different ways to solve riddles and overcome obstacles. The indie project allows you to arrange androids in different places and move between them instead of teleports. You can also directly control the chosen cyborg, sneaking past opponents or shooting at everyone in a row. Users can even collect captured enemies in order to have access to the fast selection of shells. Fields can also be disassembled to spare parts. Each robot can be pumped using disks, giving them additional tools and giving new abilities like the opportunity to fly.
