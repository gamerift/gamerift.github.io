Game Description#

Zero Hour is a multiplayer tactical first-person shooter with realistic game mechanics that replicate the behavior of real special forces units in anti-terrorist operations. The setting for the action is criminal Bangladesh, where banditry is at a high level and is not going to give up ground to law enforcement officers. The locations where clashes between groups of terrorists and special forces occur are made using real scale, which greatly helps the atmosphere and involves gamers in the gameplay. The project collects data about each session, providing statistical summaries for each participant in the fight.

The tasks of the opposing teams are standard for the genre - bandits must protect a planted bomb from being cleared or prevent special forces from taking a hostage. Lawyers need to act quickly, because time is limited, the explosive package will soon detonate, and the prisoner could be killed. To storm enemy territory, the attacking side uses shields to protect themselves from bullets, a variety of weapons and a three-dimensional map to plan the operation. At this moment, the attackers are strengthening their possessions and setting traps.

Due to the emphasis on the tactical component, the project introduces many interesting mechanics into the gameplay, for example, switches that turn off the lights in a certain area. Special agents have night vision flashlights; they will be able to see the enemy in complete darkness at the cost of giving away their location. This can be used to good advantage by diverting attention to one team member while the rest sneak past enemies. Also, doors become a serious obstacle, because there is no understanding of what is behind it and whether an ambush awaits there.
